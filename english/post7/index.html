Did you know that you have 4-6 dreams at night, which means 2000 dreams per year! Unfortunately we forget 80% of all dreams. Here are 9 fascinating things happen to your body while you sleep.

7. Your Muscles are paralyzed

When you are sleeping your muscle will be paralyzed completely. It's temporarily impossible to move.

6. Your eyes move at full speed

All phases of sleep have certain purpose & to keeping your body & brain healthy & relaxed. There are 5 stages of sleep & each one is deeper than previous:
• Falling asleep
• light sleep
• moderate sleep
• deep sleep
• Raw dreaming stage
When we pass through all 5 phases, the cycle start over again. The last one is the most active. It starts 60 or 90 minutes after you fall asleep. At this stage your eyes moves at full speed back & forth without being aware of it, because your mind focuses on your dreams.

5. A growth hormone is released

Human growth hormone known as HGH is responsible for the regeneration of your bones, muscles & tissue. When you sleep these hormone is produced all over your body. It helps to healing wounds. When you are young these hormone contributes to growth & affects your body in many other ways. Now you know, You go taller when you sleep!

4. Your throat tightens

When you sleep, the muscles of your throat relax. Therefore the size decreases, this is one of the reasons you snore. Although there are other factors which makes you snore.

3. You grind your teeth

This phenomenon is called bruxism. It doesn't happen to everyone, but some people wake up with strong jaw pain after grinding the teeth at night. Bruxism might occurs as a result misaligned jaw. It can also be psychological. However, studies have not identified why some people never experienced & others are.
